RECORDING INSTRUCTIONS

Keep it short, clean, and weird.

On a computer

  1. Download Audacity.
  2. Use one microphone in a quiet room. Stay about a hand-span away.
  3. Record the sound once or twice. Leave a little silence before and after.
  4. Trim the extra takes. Do not add reverb, pitch effects, or music.
  5. Export as WAV, 16-bit PCM, mono. Use the filename on the character card.

On iPhone or Android

  1. Use Voice Memos, Recorder, or the phone's built-in recording app.
  2. Record in a quiet, soft room and keep the phone a hand-span away.
  3. Send the original file—even if the phone saves M4A instead of WAV. It can be converted.
  4. Rename it to the filename shown on the card if your phone allows it; otherwise include the character name in the Discord message.
Best: WAV  •  Game-ready: WAV, Ogg Vorbis, MP3  •  Phone originals: accepted for conversion

A NEW ROLE-PLAYING GAME FROM THE WRONG SIDE OF THE COUNTY LINE

Welcome to Halfway, Ontario.

Population 2,417. The sign says 2,418. Nobody agrees who left.

Most RPGs ask whether you can win. This one asks whether winning was the problem.

Congratulations! You are Richard Deacon: township plow driver, elite chirper, father of two, and the only man in Halfway capable of turning a custody hearing into a boss fight.

Divorced Dad RPG is a tragicomic, turn-based adventure set in rural Ontario in 2008. Your weapons are comebacks, excuses, barstool intelligence, a flip phone, and occasionally a municipal fish. The hard part is figuring out when to put all of them down and LISTEN.

01

The fights are arguments.

Courtrooms, family blowups, bar confrontations, and rink-side disasters become tactical verbal combat. A perfect comeback can earn a giant VICTORY and still cost you something the result screen refuses to explain.

02

The town keeps receipts.

Halfway remembers what you said, what you skipped, and who watched. The house gets worse. The music changes. People start calling you Richard, Rich, or Dick. There is no glowing morality meter—just the unsettling discovery that your life has been taking notes.

03

The side quests could only happen here.

Work the arena. Survive a Legion meat draw. Tow things that should not move. Mow a lawn with the tactical importance of Normandy. Fish with methods the conservation officer would describe as “evidence.” Every ridiculous job feeds the same messy little life.

04

It is funny because it hurts.

This is not misery tourism. Halfway is full of warm, strange, painfully observant people worth showing up for. The comedy gets you through the door; the bruised heart underneath is why you will still be thinking about them after the bar closes.

No chosen one. No dragon. One dad, twenty weeks, and a town that already heard.

By lending a voice, you are not just making a text blip. You are helping Halfway become a place that can chirp, warn, forgive, remember—and absolutely tell the whole Legion what Richard did.
